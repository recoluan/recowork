# Divergence And Convergence

1. Explore directions from the problem, user, scenario, constraints, and counterexamples.
2. For every direction, state its value proposition, key assumptions, risks, and smallest validation.
3. Compare and group directions by value, evidence, feasibility, differentiation, and cost.
4. Mark unselected directions as retained, excluded, or pending validation rather than deleting their rationale.
5. Ask the user to confirm the idea agreement before selecting a priority direction.
